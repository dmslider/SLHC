24/7 assistance for Sierra Leonean citizens in distress

Emergency Consular Assistance

The High Commission provides emergency assistance to Sierra Leonean citizens in Ireland who find themselves in crisis situations. Our emergency hotline is available 24/7.

üö® Emergency Hotline

Phone: +353 XX XXX XXXX (Available 24/7)
Email: emergency@slhc-ireland.ie
For life-threatening emergencies, contact Irish emergency services (999 or 112) first.

When to Contact Emergency Services

  • Lost or stolen passport while traveling
  • Arrest or detention
  • Serious illness or hospitalization
  • Death of a Sierra Leonean citizen
  • Victim of crime or violence
  • Natural disasters or civil unrest
  • Destitution (no money or accommodation)

Emergency Travel Documents

If your passport is lost or stolen and you need to travel urgently, we can issue an Emergency Travel Document (ETD).

Requirements:

  • Police report (for lost/stolen passport)
  • Completed ETD application form
  • Two passport photographs
  • Proof of identity (birth certificate, old passport copy, ID)
  • Flight booking or travel itinerary
  • Fee: ‚Ç¨120

Processing Time: 2-3 working days (for genuine emergencies)

üìã Important Note

Emergency Travel Documents are valid for single journey only and must be used within the specified dates. You must apply for a new passport upon return to Ireland.

Arrest or Detention Assistance

If you are arrested or detained in Ireland, contact us immediately. We can:

  • Visit you in detention
  • Provide list of English-speaking lawyers
  • Contact your family in Sierra Leone
  • Monitor your welfare and treatment
  • Liaise with Irish authorities on your behalf
‚öñÔ∏è Legal Limitations

The High Commission cannot: get you out of prison, give legal advice, pay fines or legal fees, or interfere with Irish legal processes. We can only ensure you are treated fairly under Irish law.

Medical Emergencies

If you are seriously ill or hospitalized:

  • We can visit you in hospital
  • Contact your family in Sierra Leone
  • Provide information about local medical facilities
  • Help arrange medical repatriation (if needed and funded)

Note: We cannot pay medical bills or arrange travel insurance.

Death of a Sierra Leonean Citizen

If a Sierra Leonean citizen dies in Ireland, we can:

  • Notify next of kin in Sierra Leone
  • Provide information about local funeral services
  • Liaise with Irish authorities
  • Issue death certificate for registration in Sierra Leone
  • Advise on repatriation of remains

Financial Distress

If you find yourself without money or accommodation:

  • We can contact your family or friends for financial support
  • Provide information about local shelters and support services
  • In extreme cases, provide a small emergency loan (must be repaid)
üí∞ Emergency Loans

Emergency loans are only provided in exceptional circumstances and must be repaid before any future passport or consular services can be provided. Maximum loan amount: ‚Ç¨100.

What We Cannot Do

The High Commission has limitations on what assistance we can provide:

  • We cannot get you out of prison or prevent deportation
  • We cannot give you legal advice or represent you in court
  • We cannot pay your bills, fines, or legal fees
  • We cannot investigate crimes
  • We cannot provide accommodation or financial support beyond emergency loans
  • We cannot interfere in private disputes (family, business, property)

Crisis Preparedness Tips

  • Always carry a copy of your passport separately from the original
  • Register with the High Commission when you arrive in Ireland
  • Keep our emergency contact number saved in your phone
  • Purchase comprehensive travel insurance before traveling
  • Keep your family informed of your travel plans
  • Store important documents digitally (cloud or email)
