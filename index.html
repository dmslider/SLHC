About Our Mission

The High Commission of Sierra Leone in Ireland was established to represent the interests of Sierra Leone and its citizens in the Republic of Ireland. We are committed to fostering strong bilateral relations and providing excellent consular services.

Our mission extends beyond traditional diplomatic functions to include promoting trade, investment, tourism, and cultural exchanges between Sierra Leone and Ireland.

üõÇ Visa Services

Complete guide to obtaining a visa for Sierra Leone

Visa Types and Requirements

Sierra Leone offers several visa categories depending on the purpose and duration of your visit. All visa applications must be submitted in person or through our online portal by appointment only.

1. Tourist Visa (Single Entry)

Valid for tourism, visiting family, or short-term leisure travel.

  • Valid passport (minimum 6 months validity)
  • Completed visa application form
  • Two recent passport-sized photographs (white background)
  • Proof of accommodation (hotel booking or invitation letter)
  • Return flight ticket or travel itinerary
  • Bank statement (last 3 months)
  • Yellow fever vaccination certificate
  • Visa fee payment receipt

2. Business Visa

For individuals traveling to Sierra Leone for business purposes, meetings, or conferences.

  • All tourist visa requirements
  • Business invitation letter from Sierra Leone company
  • Company registration certificate (if self-employed)
  • Letter from employer stating purpose of visit
  • Proof of business relationship

3. Multiple Entry Visa

Suitable for frequent travelers who need to enter Sierra Leone multiple times within a specified period.

  • All single entry visa requirements
  • Letter explaining need for multiple entries
  • Proof of previous travel to Sierra Leone (if applicable)
  • Additional documentation as required

4. Transit Visa

For travelers passing through Sierra Leone en route to another destination.

  • Valid passport
  • Visa application form
  • One passport photograph
  • Proof of onward travel (flight ticket)
  • Visa for final destination (if required)
üìã Important Information

Processing time is typically 5-7 working days for standard applications. Express service available for urgent cases (additional fee applies). All documents must be original or certified copies.

  6. Collect Visa: Collect your passport once approved (we will notify you)

üìÑ Consular Services

Document authentication, notarization, and certification services

Consular Services

We provide a wide range of consular services to Sierra Leonean citizens and foreign nationals. All services are by appointment only.

1. Document Notarization

Official certification of documents for use in Sierra Leone or internationally.

  • Affidavits and statutory declarations
  • Powers of attorney
  • Consent letters
  • Certified copies of documents
  • Witness signatures

Fee: ‚Ç¨30 per document | Processing: Same day

2. Birth Certificate Services

Registration and certification of births for Sierra Leonean citizens.

  • Birth registration for children born abroad
  • Duplicate birth certificates
  • Birth certificate authentication

Required Documents:

  • Hospital birth notification or certificate
  • Parents' passports and IDs
  • Marriage certificate (if applicable)
  • Two passport photos of the child

Fee: ‚Ç¨50 | Processing: 2-3 weeks

3. Marriage Certificate Services

Registration and authentication of marriages.

  • Marriage certificate registration
  • Duplicate certificates
  • Certificate authentication for foreign use

Fee: ‚Ç¨60 | Processing: 2-3 weeks

4. Death Certificate Services

Registration and authentication of death certificates.

  • Death certificate registration
  • Duplicate certificates
  • Certificate authentication

Fee: ‚Ç¨50 | Processing: 2-3 weeks

5. Authentication & Legalization

Official stamp and seal for documents to be used in Sierra Leone.

  • Educational certificates and transcripts
  • Professional qualifications
  • Police clearance certificates
  • Medical certificates
  • Business documents
  • Court documents

Fee: ‚Ç¨40 per document | Processing: 3-5 days

6. Police Clearance Certificate

Certificate of good conduct for Sierra Leonean citizens.

  • Application form
  • Passport copy
  • Fingerprints (taken at mission)
  • Two passport photographs
  • Proof of residence

Fee: ‚Ç¨80 | Processing: 4-6 weeks

7. Power of Attorney

Legal authorization for someone to act on your behalf in Sierra Leone.

  • Valid passport
  • Details of attorney (full name, address, ID)
  • Clear statement of powers being granted
  • Personal appearance required

Fee: ‚Ç¨70 | Processing: Same day

üí° Processing Information

All documents must be original or certified copies. Incomplete applications will be rejected. Express services available for urgent cases (additional fee applies).

üÜò Emergency Services

24/7 assistance for Sierra Leonean citizens in distress

Emergency Consular Assistance

The High Commission provides emergency assistance to Sierra Leonean citizens in Ireland who find themselves in crisis situations. Our emergency hotline is available 24/7.

üö® Emergency Hotline

Phone: +353 XX XXX XXXX (Available 24/7)
Email: emergency@slhc-ireland.ie
For life-threatening emergencies, contact Irish emergency services (999 or 112) first.

When to Contact Emergency Services

  • Lost or stolen passport while traveling
  • Arrest or detention
  • Serious illness or hospitalization
  • Death of a Sierra Leonean citizen
  • Victim of crime or violence
  • Natural disasters or civil unrest
  • Destitution (no money or accommodation)

Emergency Travel Documents

If your passport is lost or stolen and you need to travel urgently, we can issue an Emergency Travel Document (ETD).

Requirements:

  • Police report (for lost/stolen passport)
  • Completed ETD application form
  • Two passport photographs
  • Proof of identity (birth certificate, old passport copy, ID)
  • Flight booking or travel itinerary
  • Fee: ‚Ç¨120

Processing Time: 2-3 working days (for genuine emergencies)

üìã Important Note

Emergency Travel Documents are valid for single journey only and must be used within the specified dates. You must apply for a new passport upon return to Ireland.

Arrest or Detention Assistance

If you are arrested or detained in Ireland, contact us immediately. We can:

  • Visit you in detention
  • Provide list of English-speaking lawyers
  • Contact your family in Sierra Leone
  • Monitor your welfare and treatment
  • Liaise with Irish authorities on your behalf
‚öñÔ∏è Legal Limitations

The High Commission cannot: get you out of prison, give legal advice, pay fines or legal fees, or interfere with Irish legal processes. We can only ensure you are treated fairly under Irish law.

Medical Emergencies

If you are seriously ill or hospitalized:

  • We can visit you in hospital
  • Contact your family in Sierra Leone
  • Provide information about local medical facilities
  • Help arrange medical repatriation (if needed and funded)

Note: We cannot pay medical bills or arrange travel insurance.

Death of a Sierra Leonean Citizen

If a Sierra Leonean citizen dies in Ireland, we can:

  • Notify next of kin in Sierra Leone
  • Provide information about local funeral services
  • Liaise with Irish authorities
  • Issue death certificate for registration in Sierra Leone
  • Advise on repatriation of remains

Financial Distress

If you find yourself without money or accommodation:

  • We can contact your family or friends for financial support
  • Provide information about local shelters and support services
  • In extreme cases, provide a small emergency loan (must be repaid)
üí∞ Emergency Loans

Emergency loans are only provided in exceptional circumstances and must be repaid before any future passport or consular services can be provided. Maximum loan amount: ‚Ç¨100.

What We Cannot Do

The High Commission has limitations on what assistance we can provide:

  • We cannot get you out of prison or prevent deportation
  • We cannot give you legal advice or represent you in court
  • We cannot pay your bills, fines, or legal fees
  • We cannot investigate crimes
  • We cannot provide accommodation or financial support beyond emergency loans
  • We cannot interfere in private disputes (family, business, property)

Crisis Preparedness Tips

  • Always carry a copy of your passport separately from the original
  • Register with the High Commission when you arrive in Ireland
  • Keep our emergency contact number saved in your phone
  • Purchase comprehensive travel insurance before traveling
  • Keep your family informed of your travel plans
  • Store important documents digitally (cloud or email)

üìò Passport Services

Apply for new passports, renewals, and replacements

Passport Services Overview

The High Commission provides comprehensive passport services for Sierra Leonean citizens residing in Ireland. All passport applications require an appointment and must be submitted in person.

1. New Passport Application

For first-time applicants or those whose passport has expired more than 5 years ago.

  • Completed passport application form (signed)
  • Birth certificate (original and copy)
  • Two recent passport photographs (white background, 45mm x 35mm)
  • National ID card (if available)
  • Proof of residence in Ireland (utility bill, bank statement)
  • Previous passport (if any)
  • Application fee

2. Passport Renewal

For citizens with expiring or recently expired passports (within 5 years).

  • Current/expired passport
  • Completed renewal application form
  • Two recent passport photographs
  • Proof of residence in Ireland
  • Renewal fee

3. Lost or Stolen Passport Replacement

Emergency service for citizens who have lost their passport or had it stolen.

  • Police report (filed within 48 hours of loss/theft)
  • Completed application form
  • Two passport photographs
  • Birth certificate
  • Copy of lost passport (if available)
  • Affidavit explaining circumstances
  • Replacement fee + penalty fee

4. Damaged Passport Replacement

For passports that are water-damaged, torn, or otherwise compromised.

  • Damaged passport (must be surrendered)
  • Completed application form
  • Two passport photographs
  • Explanation letter describing damage
  • Replacement fee

5. Change of Particulars

For name changes due to marriage, divorce, or other legal reasons.

  • Current passport
  • Marriage certificate or court order (original)
  • Completed application form
  • Two passport photographs
  • Service fee
üïê Processing Times

Standard processing: 6-8 weeks | Express service: 3-4 weeks (additional fee applies) | Emergency travel document: 2-3 days (for urgent travel only)

All applicants must appear in person for biometric data capture. Children under 16 must be accompanied by both parents or legal guardians. Passport photos must meet ICAO standards.

  • No headwear (except for religious reasons)
  • Good quality print, not pixelated or blurry
